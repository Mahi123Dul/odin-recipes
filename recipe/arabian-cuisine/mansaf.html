Mansaf

Description

Mansaf is a traditional Jordanian dish that consists of tender lamb cooked in a rich yogurt-based sauce, flavored with aromatic spices like cardamom and cinnamon. The dish is typically served over a bed of fragrant rice and garnished with toasted nuts and fresh parsley. Mansaf is considered the national dish of Jordan and is often served on special occasions and gatherings, symbolizing hospitality and generosity.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg lamb meat, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups plain yogurt
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 cups long-grain rice, rinsed and drained
  • 1/4 cup ghee or clarified butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley and toasted pine nuts for garnish

Steps

  1. In a large pot, heat the ghee over medium heat and saut√© the chopped onion until golden brown.
  2. Add the minced garlic, cardamom, and cinnamon to the pot, and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the lamb pieces to the pot and brown them on all sides, then pour in the water and bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the lamb simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours until tender.
  5. In a separate bowl, whisk together the yogurt with a bit of water to thin it out, then add it to the pot with the lamb.
  6. Continue to simmer the Mansaf gently for another 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens.
  7. Meanwhile, cook the rice according to package instructions until fluffy and tender.
  8. To serve, arrange the cooked rice on a large serving platter, top with the tender lamb pieces and sauce, and garnish with chopped parsley and toasted pine nuts.
  9. Enjoy your Mansaf hot with extra yogurt sauce on the side, if desired.
